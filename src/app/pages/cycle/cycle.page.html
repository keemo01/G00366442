Menstrual Cycle Understanding your cycle

Menstruation is bleeding from the vagina that happens about once a month, as a normal part of the menstrual cycle. It is also known as having a period. During this cycle, the hormones make the lining of the uterus become thicker, getting ready in case of pregnancy. Hormones also cause an egg to be released from an ovary, which is known as ovulation. Periods can be light or heavy, and the blood can range from bright red to dark brown. There might also be small clots. Some women get symptoms leading up to and during menstruation, such as: cramps or pains low in the abdomen (tummy) bloating or swelling in the abdomen constipation before the period diarrhoea when the period starts acne tiredness mood changes
